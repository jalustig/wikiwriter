## Your Task

Synthesize the section results and make a final decision.

**Verdicts:**

- **PASS** — All sections improved with no policy violations. Accept everything.
- **REVISE** — Some sections failed but the issues are specific and fixable. Send back for revision. Use this when: failures are sourcing gaps, specific factual issues, or prose problems that can be fixed in a targeted way.
- **PARTIAL_ACCEPT** — Some sections pass, some fail. Accept the passing sections, revert failing sections to original. Use this when: revision budget is exhausted (cycle 2), or some sections are good while others have fundamental problems.
- **DISCARD** — Hard policy violation that cannot be submitted at all, not even partially. Use ONLY for: severe BLP violations, egregious NPOV failures that would embarrass Wikipedia, clear copyright problems.

**CRITICAL**: Only use DISCARD for hard policy violations. Sourcing gaps, weak prose, and factual issues warrant REVISE or PARTIAL_ACCEPT — not DISCARD.

**Revision scope** (if verdict is REVISE):
- **SECTIONS** — failures are section-local; only re-draft those specific sections
- **FULL_ARTICLE** — failures indicate structural problems requiring a full rewrite

## Output Format

Return ONLY a JSON object — no preamble, no markdown fences:

{{
  "overall_verdict": "PASS|REVISE|PARTIAL_ACCEPT|DISCARD",
  "revision_scope": "SECTIONS|FULL_ARTICLE|null",
  "passing_sections": ["section1", "section2"],
  "failing_sections": ["section3"],
  "revision_instructions": ["specific instruction for section3: what needs to change and why"],
  "discard_reason": "null or specific policy violation that makes any submission unacceptable"
}}
