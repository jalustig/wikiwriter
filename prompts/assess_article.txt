Make editorial decisions about this article. Think like a senior Wikipedia editor who has reviewed thousands of articles.

**Importance** — How important is this article to Wikipedia's mission?
- VITAL: Core knowledge every encyclopedia must cover — major world figures, countries, landmark events, foundational scientific concepts
- MAJOR: Significant topic with broad reader interest and substantial content potential
- NOTABLE: Worthy of inclusion, limited depth appropriate
- MINOR: Marginal topic, stub treatment is correct

**Classification** — What kind of article is this in its current state?
- STUB: Too short, missing major content
- DEVELOPING: Present but incomplete, gaps in coverage
- COMPLETE: Good coverage, needs polish not expansion
- OVER_DETAILED: Excessive detail on minor aspects, needs pruning

**Edit scope** — Does this article need work on specific sections, or broad treatment?
- WHOLE_ARTICLE: The article has systemic problems (poor lead, missing sections, structural issues) that require broad treatment
- SPECIFIC_SECTIONS: Only specific sections need work; the rest is fine
