## Decision Phase 1: Should we edit at all?

Check these hard stops — if ANY apply, set no_edit=true:
- Article is about a living person AND has BLP policy flags or high controversy
- Caution level is CRITICAL
- Active legal threats or harassment disputes on the talk page
- Article is fully protected or has a recent arbcom sanction
- policies_and_restrictions contains anything that explicitly prohibits editing

If no_edit=true, you MUST still provide:
- A full quality and trust assessment (how reliable is this article RIGHT NOW?)
- source_trust_verdict: can a reader trust this article's claims as written?
- would_edit_sections: the 2-3 sections you WOULD fix if allowed (ranked by priority)
- no_edit_reason: plain-language explanation of what is blocking editing

## Decision Phase 2: Which 2-3 sections need work?

Pick the sections that will have the MOST IMPACT. Criteria in order:
1. Sections with the lowest quality scores that aren't already well-sourced
2. Sections that are core to understanding the topic (not trivia/appendices)
3. Sections where a targeted fix is feasible (don't pick sections needing full rewrites if effort_ceiling is LIGHT)

**Cap: select at most 3 sections for EDIT. If there are more candidates, pick the top 3 and SKIP the rest.**

Do NOT edit: flip-flopped sections, "References", "External links", "See also", "Notes" (unless they are the only meaningful content).

**Importance:**
- VITAL: Core knowledge every encyclopedia must cover
- MAJOR: Significant, broad interest
- NOTABLE: Worthy of inclusion, limited depth
- MINOR: Marginal, stub treatment correct

**article_class:**
- STUB: Too short, missing major content
- DEVELOPING: Present but incomplete
- COMPLETE: Good coverage, needs polish
- OVER_DETAILED: Excessive detail, needs pruning

**effort_ceiling:**
- FULL: Deep research warranted
- MODERATE: Targeted improvements
- LIGHT: Minor fixes only

**edit_type per section:**
- EXPAND: thin or missing content
- FACT_CHECK: suspicious, outdated, or contested claims
- PRUNE: excessive or off-topic detail
- CITE_REPAIR: missing or broken citations
